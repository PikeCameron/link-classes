CSS Topic - Link Pseudo-classes

Using CSS, you can change the appearance of your links whether you hover your mouse over them, if they were visited or not, or even what happens when you first click on the link. The magic of links is right at your finger tips!

:link

The best place to start is by deciding how you want your link to look before anything happens. To alter your link right off the bat, you will use a:link. You start with a because every link you use will be within an a tag.

See the Pen CSS Link Pseudo-Classes by PikeCameron (@pikecameron) on CodePen.

*Remember to use href="" in your a tag to include your link. Here the link leads somewhere they can buy more dog food like petco!

:visited

Contrary to :link, :visited is how the your link looks after it has already been clicked on.

See the Pen Untitled by PikeCameron (@pikecameron) on CodePen.

Here Tesla changes red after it has been visited by the user.

:hover

Okay. Time to put on your big boy trousers because now it gets good. a:hover is something you can do in CSS to change how the link interacts with your mouse when you hover over the link.

Hover sometimes proves problematic on touch devices. Hover works by a mouse or pointer floating over the element but touch devices don't have a pointer. Often times to get around this, touch software will have the user tap the link which will then show the hover element, and then tap it again to follow the link.

See the Pen CSS Link :hover by PikeCameron (@pikecameron) on CodePen.

Here we see the hover effect over "walk on water" because we all know only Jesus could walk on water.

:active

:active is similar to :hover in the way the link reacts. The results are very short but happens in the moment you click on the link. Here the link is bolded and turns pink when you click on it and quickly reverts back. If you were to have a :visited tag, it would take on those effects after it has been clicked.
